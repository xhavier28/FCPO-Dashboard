EXPLANATION OF LOW "Current" COVERAGE:
  The total bar count includes all bars from all contract files. Contracts
  begin trading 6-12 months before expiry. A bar from Jun24's file dated
  in early 2023 has front_month in 2023, but no 2023 contracts exist in
  our dataset (earliest = Jan24). Thus "Current" can only be filled when
  front_month(date) >= Jan24, i.e. from ~Jan 2024 onward.

  Additionally, butterfly contracts stop trading before the 15th of their
  near-leg month (e.g. Jan24 butterfly last bar = Jan 9, 2024), creating
  a structural gap at month-end where the "Current" file has expired but
  +1M has not yet become "Current" (roll happens on day 16).

  For Spread, the pattern is similar but less severe since spread contracts
  trade slightly longer near expiry.

  These are structural features of the instrument lifecycle, NOT data errors.

--- Part 1.4: Butterfly Current Still Below 90% — Investigation ---

Butterfly "Current" coverage = 71.2% (179 missing dates out of 621).

ROOT CAUSE: Butterfly contracts expire (stop trading) significantly EARLIER
than the front_month roll rule assumes. Expiry dates for butterfly files:

  Contract   Last Trading Date   Days Before Month-End
  Jan24      2024-01-09          day 9
  Feb24      2024-02-05          day 5
  Mar24      2024-03-14          day 14
  Apr24      2024-04-05          day 5
  May24      2024-05-14          day 14
  Jun24      2024-06-10          day 10
  (typical range: day 3-14)

The front_month rule assigns "Current" = a given month from day 16 of prior
month through day 15 of that month. But the butterfly typically stops trading
by day 3-14 of its month, creating a structural gap of ~1-12 days at the END
of each "Current" window where no data exists because the instrument has
expired. This is NOT a data-sourcing gap — it's the instrument's actual
trading lifecycle.

Missing dates are ALL in the last ~1-12 days of each contract's "Current"
window, confirming the mechanism. The +1M tenor (which maps to the NEXT
contract that's still actively trading) has 100% coverage.

--- Part 2: Butterfly vs Spread Coverage Gap Mechanism ---

FINDING: The "missing far leg" hypothesis from the task spec does NOT apply.
Both Butterfly and Spread files contain PRE-COMPUTED composite prices (the
exchange/platform already computed near - 2*mid + far for butterfly, and
near - far for spread). We do not have separate leg files.

The coverage gap between Butterfly and Spread is caused by TWO structural
differences in the instruments themselves:

  1. EARLIER EXPIRY: Butterfly contracts stop trading 2-10 days earlier
     than Spread contracts for the same near-leg month.
     Examples:
       Feb24: Butterfly last bar = Feb 5, Spread last bar = Feb 14 (9 days gap)
       Apr24: Butterfly last bar = Apr 5, Spread last bar = Apr 15 (10 days gap)
       Aug24: Butterfly last bar = Aug 7, Spread last bar = Aug 9 (2 days gap)
       Dec24: Butterfly last bar = Dec 3, Spread last bar = Dec 13 (10 days gap)

  2. FEWER BARS PER DAY: Butterfly averages 5.0-6.5 bars/day (60-min bars)
     vs Spread averaging 7.2-7.9 bars/day. This explains the "by-bar" gap
     but NOT the "by-date" gap (which is purely mechanism #1).

Dates where Spread "Current" has data but Butterfly "Current" does not:
  128 dates (all caused by butterfly having already expired while spread
  still trades). Example:
    2024-01-10: front_month=Jan24, spread Jan24 still trading, butterfly Jan24 expired (last=Jan 9)
    2024-02-06: front_month=Feb24, spread Feb24 still trading, butterfly Feb24 expired (last=Feb 5)

For +1M through +9M: ZERO dates where Spread has data but Butterfly doesn't
(on a by-date basis). The gap is exclusively in the "Current" tenor due to
the expiry timing difference.

PART 2.3 (far-leg-only missing %): NOT APPLICABLE. Since files are pre-
computed composites, we cannot decompose into "near leg present, far leg
missing" vs "multiple legs missing". The entire instrument simply stops
trading. 100% of Butterfly's missing "Current" dates are due to the
instrument having expired, not a missing individual leg.
